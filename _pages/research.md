My research lies at the intersection of **applied algebraic topology**, **topological data analysis**, and **topological robotics**. A central theme of my work is understanding how the topology of a space changes when several geometric or physical parameters vary simultaneously.

## Configuration spaces of metric graphs

One of the main objects I study is the filtration of restricted configuration spaces of metric graphs. These spaces can be interpreted as configuration spaces for multiple robots moving along a finite rail network while satisfying prescribed separation constraints.

Using tools from topological data analysis, I investigate how topological features of these configuration spaces change as the restraint parameter and edge-length parameters vary. My thesis focused on bifiltrations associated with restricted second configuration spaces of finite metric trees.

## Multiparameter persistent homology

Multiparameter persistence arises naturally when a data set or filtered space depends on more than one parameter. In contrast with one-parameter persistence, the multiparameter setting generally has no barcode classification or complete structure theorem. My work studies concrete persistence modules, finite encodings, decompositions, and compatible cycle representatives.

The techniques I use include representations of partially ordered sets, homological algebra, category theory, combinatorial topology, and discrete Morse theory.

## Directed topology

I also study metric and topological constructions for directed spaces. In joint work, we introduced a directed analogue of Gromov--Hausdorff distance and investigated formulations based on directed maps and directed correspondences.
